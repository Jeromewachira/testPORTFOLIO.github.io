About Me

As a BSc Electronics graduate, I've always been fascinated by the intersection of hardware and software systems. My academic journey provided me with a solid foundation in circuit design, embedded systems, and programming, but it was during my studies that I discovered my true passion: cybersecurity.

The rapid digitization of our world and the increasing sophistication of cyber threats have motivated me to transition into cybersecurity. My electronics background gives me a unique perspective on understanding hardware-software interfaces, which is crucial in identifying vulnerabilities that others might overlook.

I'm particularly drawn to network security, ethical hacking, and system protection. My analytical thinking, problem-solving skills, and attention to detail—honed through years of working with complex electronic systems—translate perfectly to identifying and mitigating security risks.

Currently, I'm actively pursuing industry certifications like CompTIA Security+ and Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH), while building hands-on experience through practical projects and home labs. My goal is to contribute to organizations that prioritize digital security and help build a safer digital ecosystem for everyone.
